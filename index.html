Can you do offset printing?

Offset printing is the process by which an image is transferred (or “offset”) in a metallic way to a rubber blanket or roller and then to the printed surface. Also known as offset lithography, this process is ideal for large productions such as newspapers, magazines, catalogs and books.

The printed media itself, usually paper, never comes into contact with metal plates, which prolongs the life of the plates. The rubber blankets/rolls correspond to the surface of the printed media, giving a clear final image. This also allows offset printers to print on canvas, plastic, wood or fabric.

At Printing New York, we use roll Offset Printing, which is a larger, high-speed machine that feeds paper reels through the printer; printing is done continuously as the paper arrives. After printing, the pages are separated and cut. Offset printing on the web is very cost-effective for high-volume publications whose content may vary, for example, in newspapers.

What are some tips for artwork files?

  • Use CMYK color mode to prepare your files, not RGB.

  • Set the font color to 100% K instead of 4c for black text.

  • Enter the desired Pantone or HKS colors in to the color channel when using special colors

  • Instructions for special colors: Create areas with another process color (CMYK) overlaying a special color using knockout instead of overprinting. Special colors over a CMYK color should be created using knockout.

  • Use greyscale color mode when ordering a greyscale item.

  • Create grey areas in shades of black to ensure a perfect print result in greyscale
